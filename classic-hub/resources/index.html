TV Shows
British and American TV restoration and classic car shows — where to watch them and which episodes are worth seeking out.
WD
Wheeler Dealers
Discovery+ / Motor Trend
🇬🇧 · 2003–present
Mike Brewer finds undervalued classics; Edd China (the woolly hat) fixes them. The definitive British classic car restoration format. British cars — including Triumph TRs — appear regularly. Edd China left after Series 13 but the early seasons with him are essential viewing.
📺 Where to watch: Discovery+, Motor Trend app, older episodes on YouTube
Our take: Essential — the benchmark for accessible classic car restoration TV
FLC
For the Love of Cars
Channel 4 (UK)
🇬🇧 · 2014–2015
Philip Glenister and Ant Anstead sourcing and restoring significant classic cars. More passionate and less formulaic than Wheeler Dealers. Beautiful cars, excellent restoration work. Jaguar E-Type and Aston Martin featured prominently.
📺 Where to watch: Channel 4 All 4 (streaming), occasional repeats
Our take: Highly recommended — more cinematic and emotionally engaging than most car TV
RH
Richard Hammond's Workshop
Discovery+
🇬🇧 · 2020–present
Richard Hammond (of Top Gear and The Grand Tour) restores classics in his Welsh workshop. Less polished production than big-budget shows but genuinely personal — this is Hammond's own passion project. Covers various British and European classics.
📺 Where to watch: Discovery+ (streaming)
Our take: Good — the personal passion makes it more authentic than most; production can be rough
SH
Salvage Hunters: Classic Cars
Quest / Discovery+
🇬🇧 · 2016–present
Paul Cowland sources classic cars for Drew Pritchard's customers. More buying/dealing than deep restoration but covers a huge range of British classics. Good for understanding the buying process and what to look for. Triumph and Jaguar content appears regularly.
📺 Where to watch: Quest channel, Discovery+
Our take: Good — practical buying knowledge, less workshop depth
B&C
Bangers & Cash
Channel 5 (UK)
🇬🇧 · 2019–present
Following the Mathewson's classic car auction house in North Yorkshire. Real auction drama — cars arriving, being assessed, selling. Not restoration-focused but excellent for understanding classic car values and what buyers actually pay. TR and Jaguar content is regular.
📺 Where to watch: Channel 5, My5 streaming
Our take: Excellent for market reality — what cars actually sell for, not what people hope for
JLG
Jay Leno's Garage (TV series)
CNBC (US)
🇺🇸 · 2015–present
The TV version of Jay's YouTube channel — more produced, with guests and themed episodes. Covers classics from all over the world. E-Types, XKs and various Triumphs appear. Jay's encyclopaedic knowledge is the show's strongest asset.
📺 Where to watch: CNBC, Peacock streaming (US), clips on YouTube
Our take: Good — best for the US audience; the British classic content is excellent
CC
Counting Cars
History Channel (US)
🇺🇸 · 2012–2022
Danny Koker's Count's Kustoms shop in Las Vegas. More hot rod and custom-focused than pure classic but occasionally covers British cars. The restoration business economics are interesting even when the cars are not your taste.
📺 Where to watch: History Channel, available on streaming
Our take: Background viewing — not specifically relevant to TR/Jaguar but entertaining

Websites & Forums
Technical resources, forums, classifieds and heritage databases. The free ones are genuinely invaluable — particularly Jag-lovers and Buckeye Triumphs.
JL
Jag-lovers.com
🇺🇸 / 🌐 · Forum Free
The original Jaguar technical forum — founded in the 1990s and still one of the most comprehensive free technical resources for Jaguar owners anywhere. Massive archive of technical discussions covering E-Type, XK, Mk II, XJ and all classic Jaguar models. Slow but priceless.
💡 Best for: Deep technical questions — the archive has answers to problems you haven't encountered yet
Visit jag-lovers.com →
BT
Buckeye Triumphs
🇺🇸 · Technical Free
A remarkable free technical resource maintained by enthusiasts. Comprehensive wiring diagrams, restoration guides, parts references and technical notes for all Triumph TR models. The PI injection section alone is worth the bookmark. Essential for TR5 and TR6 owners.
💡 Best for: TR5/TR6 PI injection — wiring diagrams and restoration guides for all TR models
Visit buckeyetriumphs.org →
HM
Hemmings Motor News
🇺🇸 · Resource Free
America's premier classic car resource — classifieds, news, buying guides and an excellent editorial archive. The Hemmings blog has well-researched articles on Triumph and Jaguar history. Good for US market pricing context.
💡 Best for: US market pricing and history articles — classifieds have good US-spec TR and Jaguar listings
Visit hemmings.com →
BaT
Bring a Trailer (BaT)
🇺🇸 · Auction Free
The most influential classic car auction platform in the world. Every E-Type and TR sale has a comment thread with knowledgeable buyers picking over the details — more valuable than the listing itself. Search past sales for realistic price data.
💡 Best for: Price research — search past sales of your specific model; the comments are a free inspection
Visit bringatrailer.com →
TRR
TR Register Technical
🇬🇧 · Technical Free
The TR Register's technical section — freely accessible even to non-members. Wiring diagrams, technical bulletins, known problems and solutions for all TR models. The member forum adds significantly more depth.
💡 Best for: TR-specific technical issues — the known problems section is particularly useful when buying
Visit tr-register.co.uk →
JDHT
Jaguar Daimler Heritage Trust
🇬🇧 · Heritage Subscription
The official custodian of Jaguar's heritage. Heritage Certificates (factory build records) are issued here — essential for establishing provenance on E-Types and XKs. Also runs the Jaguar Heritage collection at the British Motor Museum.
💡 Best for: Heritage Certificates — if you're buying an E-Type, insist on one. If it doesn't exist, apply here.
Visit jdht.com →

BMIHT
BMIHT — British Motor Industry Heritage Trust
🇬🇧 · Heritage Certificates ~£50/cert
Heritage Certificates for all BMC, British Leyland and Jaguar cars — confirming original factory specification, paint code, trim colour and build date from the factory archives. Available for most Austin-Healey, MG, Triumph and Jaguar models. A Heritage Certificate is essential proof for concours judging and originality claims. For any serious restoration, one is non-negotiable.
💡 If you're buying an Austin-Healey, MG, Triumph or Jaguar — insist on a Heritage Certificate, or budget ~£50 to apply for one yourself
Visit heritage.org.uk →
