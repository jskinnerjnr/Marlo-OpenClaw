Best-sellingBritish sports car
Overview

The MGB is the best-selling British sports car of all time — 523,836 built over eighteen years at Abingdon. Unlike the body-on-frame MGA, the MGB uses unitary monocoque construction with wind-up windows from the start. It ran through chrome bumper (1962–1974) and rubber bumper (1974–1980) eras, spawned the GT fastback and the rare MGC and GT V8 variants, and remained essentially unchanged for most of its life. Its simplicity makes it one of the most accessible classics to own and restore.

ⓘ Chrome bumper cars (pre-October 1974) are the most desirable. Rubber bumper cars sit 1 inch higher and are less elegant — though many have been lowered back to chrome bumper ride height. Always check the suspension geometry if this has been done.

Factory Body Colours

Paint codes are BMC codes (pre-1968) or British Leyland BLVC codes (from 1968). BLVC codes are the most comprehensively documented and apply to the majority of MGBs built. Hex swatches are digital approximations only. Always verify original colour via a Heritage Certificate from heritage.org.uk before restoration work.

⚠ Hex colour swatches are digital approximations only and should not be used for colour matching. BLVC codes listed as confirmed (BLVC 11, 14, 32, 36, 42, 52, 55, 56) are from widely cited MG technical references and club registers. Codes marked “Not confirmed” require verification against a Heritage Certificate or primary MGCC/MGOC documentation.

MGC (1967–69): The MGC shared the MGB colour palette for its production years. No MGC-exclusive colours have been documented from primary sources.
MGB GT V8 (1973–76): The factory V8 was offered in a restricted range. Confirmed available colours include Citron (BLVC 32), Bronze Yellow (BLVC 36), Glacier White (BLVC 11) and BRG (BLVC 14). Blaze (BLVC 52) and Tundra also cited. Fewer options than standard MGB.
Interior Trim Colours

The MGB used Ambla vinyl as standard throughout production; genuine leather was not a factory option. Trim colour changed significantly across eras. The original trim code was recorded on the car’s commission plate (on the slam panel under the bonnet).

Verify Your Car’s Original Spec

BMIHT Heritage Certificate

The British Motor Industry Heritage Trust holds the original build records for all MG cars. A Heritage Certificate confirms your car’s original paint colour, trim specification and factory options — definitive proof for concours, insurance and provenance. Essential before any serious restoration.
