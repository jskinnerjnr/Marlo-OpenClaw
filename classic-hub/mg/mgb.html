MG MGB

Best-sellingBritish sports car
Overview

The MGB is the best-selling British sports car of all time — 523,836 built over eighteen years at Abingdon. Unlike the body-on-frame MGA, the MGB uses unitary monocoque construction with wind-up windows from the start. It ran through chrome bumper (1962–1974) and rubber bumper (1974–1980) eras, spawned the GT fastback and the rare MGC and GT V8 variants, and remained essentially unchanged for most of its life. Its simplicity makes it one of the most accessible classics to own and restore.

ⓘ Chrome bumper cars (pre-October 1974) are the most desirable. Rubber bumper cars sit 1 inch higher and are less elegant — though many have been lowered back to chrome bumper ride height. Always check the suspension geometry if this has been done.

What to look for when buying
AreaWhat to checkSeverity
Sills (inner & outer)The MGB sills rot from both sides simultaneously. The inner sill is structural. Probe carefully — filler and paint hide extensive rot.High
Battery boxesUnder the rear deck — both battery compartments rust badly. Check carefully with a torch.High
Floor & front valanceFloor pans and front valance rot extensively. Check under carpets.High
A-posts & door bottomsA-post corrosion and door bottom rot — common and structural.Medium
Lucas electricsCheck all switches, gauges and overdrive function. Wiring looms deteriorate.Medium
OverdriveLaycock overdrive — check engagement. Solenoid, isolator switch and pump failures common. Check oil level in overdrive unit separately.Medium
Brake servo (later cars)Later cars have servo brakes — check function and pipe condition.Lower
MGC torsion barsMGC-specific: check front torsion bar condition and suspension geometry.Medium (MGC only)
