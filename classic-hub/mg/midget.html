MG Midget

1961–1979Production years
~226,000Total produced

The Spridget — shared DNA with the Sprite
ⓘ The Midget shares its DNA entirely with the Austin-Healey Sprite — see our Sprite page for the full shared story, including the Frogeye Mk1 history, technical evolution and complete buying guide. This page covers the Midget-specific details and final 1500 variant.

The MG Midget launched simultaneously with the Mk2 Austin-Healey Sprite in 1961. Identical mechanically, it wore an MG grille, better interior trim and a slightly higher price tag. Both evolved together until the Sprite was discontinued in 1971; the Midget continued to 1979, latterly with the controversial Triumph 1500 engine. The last Midget rolled off the Abingdon line on 7 December 1979 — the last car built at the famous works.

⚠ Midget 1500 timing chain: The Triumph 1493cc engine has a known timing chain tensioner issue — always verify service history and listen carefully at cold start for chain rattle.

Interior Trim Colours
