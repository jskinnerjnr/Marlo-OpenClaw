Triumph GT6

1966–1973Production years
40,926Total produced
2.0L I6Engine
MichelottiDesign
Overview

The Triumph GT6 is effectively a Spitfire with a six-cylinder engine and a fastback coupe roofline — taking the Spitfire platform and fitting it with the 1996cc straight-six from the Vitesse saloon. Styled by Michelotti with a distinctive Kamm tail and long bonnet, it was immediately nicknamed the "poor man's E-Type." Built at Canley, Coventry, it ran through three marks from 1966 to 1973.

ⓘ The Mk1 GT6 is a very different car from the Mk2 and Mk3. The original swing axle rear gave the car a dangerous reputation — particularly in the USA. The Mk2's revised rear suspension transformed the handling. Always check which variant you're buying.

The GT6 shared its colour palette with the contemporary Spitfire and TR6. From Mk2 onwards, BLVC codes applied. Mk1 (1966–1968) used earlier Triumph Standard codes.
