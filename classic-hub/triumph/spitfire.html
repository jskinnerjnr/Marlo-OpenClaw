Triumph Spitfire

1962–1980Production years
315,000Total produced

MichelottiDesign
Overview

The Triumph Spitfire was designed on a shortened version of the Triumph Herald backbone chassis — body-on-frame construction that gives the car its characteristic front-hinged clamshell bonnet opening. Styled by Giovanni Michelotti and built at Canley, Coventry, it was Triumph's direct answer to the Austin-Healey Sprite and MG Midget — though unlike its rivals, it offered wind-down windows from the very first car. Named after the Supermarine Spitfire of World War Two. Five distinct marks were produced over eighteen years.

ⓘ The Spitfire's one-piece front opening is both its most striking feature and its most useful — access to the engine is exceptional. It also has one of the finest Italian-designed bodies on any British sports car of the era.
