TR7 vs TR8

The TR7 was British Leyland's wedge-shaped successor to the TR6 — controversial at launch but now well regarded. The TR8 is essentially a TR7 fitted with the Rover/Buick 3.5-litre V8, giving effortless performance. TR8 production was limited and US-market only; genuine examples are rare and valuable. Both models were built at Speke (Liverpool) and later Canley (Coventry) before the final years at Solihull.

💡 TR8 identification: TR8s carry the engine code suffix "V8" in their VIN. All TR8s are convertibles — no fixed-head TR8 was officially produced, though a few prototypes exist.

Interior trim colours

TR7 interiors were predominantly black vinyl. Beige was an option. TR8 offered leather in black, beige, and russet. Specific BLVC trim codes for the TR7/TR8 have not been confirmed from available primary sources — research ongoing.
