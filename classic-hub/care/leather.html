Leather Care

Classic British cars used Connolly hide — a firm, natural-grain leather quite different from modern synthetic materials. It responds well to the right treatment and poorly to the wrong one. Here's what actually works.

Always clean before conditioning. Connolly hide needs a pH-neutral cleaner — not household soap or washing-up liquid, which strip the oils from the leather.

2. Conditioning

Connolly hide needs regular conditioning to prevent cracking — especially on cars stored in heated garages, where low humidity dries leather faster than you'd expect. Condition two to three times per year as a minimum.

3. Touch-Up & Colour Restoration

Small scuffs, faded colour on bolsters and worn edges can often be resolved without a full retrim. Touch-up dye works best on minor damage — for serious wear or cracking, new leather is the better answer.

⚠ Original Connolly hide colours don't always match modern swatches exactly. Always test on a hidden area first — under the seat squab or on the back of a seat bolster.
