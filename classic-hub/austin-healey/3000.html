Overview

The Austin-Healey 3000 is the definitive Big Healey — a six-cylinder evolution of the 100/6 that became one of Britain's most successful export sports cars. Built at Abingdon (the MG Works) with bodies by Jensen, it ran through four major marks over eight years. Most were exported to the USA, where the Healey name was synonymous with affordable British sports car excitement. Pat Moss won the 1960 Liège-Sofia-Liège rally outright — the last major international rally won by a woman until 1990.

ⓘ Most desirable: Mk3 BJ8 Phase 2 (1965–67) — walnut dashboard, 148bhp, wind-up windows and the most refined specification of any Big Healey.

Factory Body Colours

Paint codes shown are Austin/BMC manufacturer codes where confirmed. Hex swatches are digital approximations only — colours vary significantly between batches and over time. Always verify the original specification via a Heritage Certificate from heritage.org.uk before undertaking restoration work.

Two-tone paint was a factory option throughout 3000 production. The most common combination was Colorado Red over Old English White. Primrose Yellow was introduced for the BJ8 in 1964 and became iconic on late cars.
Colour Name Years BMC Code Approx Swatch Notes
Colorado Red 1959–1967 Not confirmed
The definitive Big Healey colour. A strong, clear red. Available as solid or combined with Old English White in two-tone. Available throughout production.
Reno Red 1959–1962 Not confirmed
Brighter orange-red carried over from the 100. Dropped in favour of Colorado Red from Mk2 BJ7 onwards. Sources differ on exact availability.
Healey Blue (Ice Blue) 1959–1967 Not confirmed
The light powder/ice blue carried over from the 100. One of the most evocative colours on a Big Healey. Available throughout production.
Old English White (Ivory) 1959–1967 WT.3
Warm off-white. Frequently used as the upper or lower colour in two-tone combinations. BMC code WT.3 widely cited across BMC models of the era.
British Racing Green 1959–1967 Not confirmed
A deep, traditional BRG. Popular with the rally-minded heritage of the car. Available throughout production. Some sources list as "Dark Racing Green".
Black 1961–1967 BK.1
Standard BMC black. Less common on the 3000 than on earlier models. Often combined with red interior on later BJ8 cars.
Golden Beige 1961–1967 Not confirmed
A warm golden-tan. Popular from BJ7 onwards. Often paired with beige interior on later BJ8 Phase 2 cars.
Primrose Yellow 1964–1967 Not confirmed
Introduced with the BJ8. A bright, warm yellow that became synonymous with late Big Healeys. Highly sought-after today. Typically paired with black interior.

Two-tone factory options: Colorado Red / Old English White · Healey Blue / Old English White · BRG / Old English White · Golden Beige / Old English White. Two-tone required the appropriate factory option order — not all combinations were available on all variants.

Interior Trim Colours

Interior trim codes for Austin-Healey cars are less systematically documented than some contemporaries. The following is compiled from multiple sources including club registers, workshop manuals, and Heritage Certificates. Material specification upgraded progressively from leathercloth on early cars to higher-quality leather on the BJ8.

Trim Colour Variants Trim Code Material Notes
Black Mk1, Mk2, BJ7, BJ8 Not confirmed Leathercloth (Mk1–BJ7); leather (BJ8) Available throughout production. Paired with most exterior colours. Standard on Primrose Yellow BJ8 cars.
Red Mk1, Mk2, BJ7, BJ8 Not confirmed Leathercloth (Mk1–BJ7); leather (BJ8) Available throughout. Standard pairing with Colorado Red exterior. Also available with Old English White and Black exteriors.
Beige BJ8 only Not confirmed Leather (Phase 1 & 2) Introduced with the Mk3 BJ8. Paired with Golden Beige exterior. A more civilised, touring-oriented option reflecting the BJ8’s grand touring character.
ⓘ BJ8 Phase 2 (from late 1965): The addition of a walnut veneer dashboard is the most significant distinguishing feature of Phase 2 cars. This — combined with the higher-quality leather trim — makes the Phase 2 the most desirable and valuable of all 3000 variants.
