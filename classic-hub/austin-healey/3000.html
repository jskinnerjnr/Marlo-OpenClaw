Overview

The Austin-Healey 3000 is the definitive Big Healey — a six-cylinder evolution of the 100/6 that became one of Britain's most successful export sports cars. Built at Abingdon (the MG Works) with bodies by Jensen, it ran through four major marks over eight years. Most were exported to the USA, where the Healey name was synonymous with affordable British sports car excitement. Pat Moss won the 1960 Liège-Sofia-Liège rally outright — the last major international rally won by a woman until 1990.

ⓘ Most desirable: Mk3 BJ8 Phase 2 (1965–67) — walnut dashboard, 148bhp, wind-up windows and the most refined specification of any Big Healey.
