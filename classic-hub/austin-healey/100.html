Overview

The Austin-Healey 100 began life as the "Healey Hundred" — a prototype shown at the 1952 Earls Court Motor Show. Leonard Lord of Austin was so taken with it that he struck a deal overnight, and the Austin-Healey brand was born. Named for its ability to exceed 100mph in standard trim, it was built at Longbridge with bodies supplied by Jensen Motors of West Bromwich. Over 91% were exported, primarily to the United States.

ⓘ The Austin A90 Atlantic engine was adapted for the 100 — a 2660cc four-cylinder producing 90bhp in standard BN1 form.

⚠ 100M vs 100S: Many cars have been "converted" to 100M specification retrospectively. Only ~640 were factory-built. Verify provenance carefully — genuine factory 100Ms carry a significant premium.
Factory Body Colours

Paint codes shown are Austin/BMC manufacturer codes where confirmed. Hex swatches are digital approximations only — colours vary significantly between batches and over time. Always verify the original specification via a Heritage Certificate from heritage.org.uk before undertaking restoration work.

Two-tone options were introduced with the BN2 in 1955. Single-colour cars predominate on BN1s. The 100S (1955, 50 cars) was offered predominantly in Ivory/Old English White with a red interior.
Colour Name Variants BMC Code Approx Swatch Notes
Healey Blue (Ice Blue) BN1, BN2 Not confirmed
The signature launch colour. A powder/ice blue — light and distinctive. One of the most common BN1 colours.
Carmine Red BN1, BN2 Not confirmed
A deep, warm crimson-red. Standard with red leathercloth interior. Also called "Healey Red" informally.
Reno Red BN1, BN2 Not confirmed
Brighter, more orange-red than Carmine. Carried over to the 3000.
Spruce Green BN1, BN2 Not confirmed
A deep, traditional British racing-adjacent dark green.
Florida Green BN2 Not confirmed
A lighter, brighter green added for BN2. Popular with US export market.
Old English White (Ivory) BN1, BN2, 100S WT.3
Warm off-white. Standard colour for the 100S competition model. Used as upper colour in BN2 two-tones. BMC code WT.3 widely cited across BMC models of the era.
Black BN2 BK.1
Added for BN2. Used as lower body in two-tone combinations. Standard BMC black.

Two-tone combinations (BN2 only): Old English White over Black · Healey Blue over Old English White · Reno Red over Black. Upper body listed first.

Interior Trim Colours

Interior trim codes for Austin-Healey cars are less systematically documented than some contemporaries. The following is compiled from multiple sources including club registers, workshop manuals, and Heritage Certificates. The 100 used leathercloth throughout — genuine leather was not factory-standard.

Verify Your Car's Original Specification

BMIHT Heritage Certificate

The British Motor Industry Heritage Trust holds the original build records for Austin-Healey cars. A Heritage Certificate (~£50) confirms your car's original paint colour, trim specification, build date and factory options — the definitive document for concours judging and provenance verification. Essential for verifying a genuine 100M or 100S.
