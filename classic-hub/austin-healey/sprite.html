Overview — the Spridget story

The Austin-Healey Sprite and MG Midget share a single platform and are collectively known among enthusiasts as the "Spridget." Both were designed by Donald Healey's team (styling by Gerry Coker and Geoffrey Healey) and built at BMC's Abingdon factory. The MG Midget was launched simultaneously with the Mk2 Sprite in 1961 — sharing identical mechanicals but wearing MG badging, a different grille, and slightly better trim at a small premium. The two models evolved in lockstep until the Sprite was discontinued in 1971; the Midget continued until 1979 with the controversial Triumph-engined 1500 variant.

ⓘ The Mk1 Frogeye/Bugeye is one of the most characterful British sports cars ever made. The headlight retraction mechanism was cut from the design to save cost — the headlights simply sit on top of the bonnet, giving the car its distinctive frog-eyed look.

Factory Body Colours — Mk1 Frogeye (1958–1961)

Paint codes shown are Austin/BMC manufacturer codes where confirmed. Hex swatches are digital approximations only — colours vary significantly between batches and over time. Always verify the original specification via a Heritage Certificate from heritage.org.uk before undertaking restoration work.

The Frogeye was offered in a limited initial colour range at launch. Primrose Yellow is the most iconic and sought-after Frogeye colour. The Sprite used standard BMC/Austin colour codes shared with other models of the era.
Colour Name Years BMC Code Approx Swatch Notes
Primrose Yellow 1958–1961 Not confirmed
The most iconic Frogeye colour — a bright, warm yellow that perfectly suits the car’s cheerful character. Available from launch. Commands a premium today.
Tartan Red 1958–1961 Not confirmed
A standard BMC red available on the Frogeye from launch. Bright and bold. One of the original palette colours.
Carmine Red 1958–1961 Not confirmed
A deeper, darker red than Tartan Red. Also available on the 100. Some sources list Tartan Red and Carmine Red as effectively the same colour in this era — sources differ.
Ice Blue (Healey Blue) 1958–1961 Not confirmed
The powder blue shared across the Austin-Healey range. Available on the Frogeye throughout its production run.
Leaf Green 1958–1961 Not confirmed
A mid-green — lighter than British Racing Green. Cheerful and well-suited to the Frogeye’s character. Not all sources confirm this as a separate option from the greens below.
British Racing Green 1958–1961 Not confirmed
The traditional deep dark green. Available on the Frogeye. Less common than Primrose Yellow or the reds.
Old English White 1958–1961 WT.3
Warm off-white. A standard BMC colour across many models. BMC code WT.3 widely cited.
Black 1958–1961 BK.1
Standard BMC black. Available on the Frogeye. Less common than the bright colours. No two-tone option on Mk1.
Factory Body Colours — Mk2–Mk4 Sprite / MG Midget (1961–1971)

From the Mk2, the Sprite and MG Midget shared identical colour availability. The palette expanded over the 1960s to follow BMC’s broader colour range. Many colours are shared across the BMC/BL family (Mini, 1100, MGC, etc.) of the same period.

Colour Name Approx Years BMC Code Approx Swatch Notes
Tartan Red 1961–1967 Not confirmed
Continued from Mk1. Standard red option on Mk2 and Mk3.
Flame Red 1967–1971 Not confirmed
A brighter, more vivid red used on later Mk4 Sprites and Midgets. Part of BL’s later colour range.
Primrose Yellow 1961–1971 Not confirmed
Continued from Mk1 into the later Spridgets. Remained popular throughout the run.
Old English White 1961–1971 WT.3
Continued throughout. BMC code WT.3.
British Racing Green 1961–1971 Not confirmed
Continued throughout. One of the more popular non-red colours on Mk2 and Mk3.
Riviera Blue 1964–1971 Not confirmed
A richer, deeper blue replacing or supplementing Ice Blue from the mid-1960s. Used across multiple BMC models.
Iris Blue 1967–1971 Not confirmed
Used on Mk4 Sprites and later Midgets. A medium blue with a slightly purplish hue.
Black 1961–1971 BK.1
Standard BMC black. Available throughout. Less common than the bright colours.

Note: The Spridget colour range changed frequently, following BMC/BL’s corporate palette updates. Not all colours were available in all years. The above list represents typical confirmed options; club registers and Heritage Certificates are the definitive source.

Interior Trim Colours

Interior trim codes for Austin-Healey Sprites are less systematically documented than some contemporaries. The following is compiled from multiple sources including club registers, workshop manuals, and Heritage Certificates.

Trim Colour Marks Trim Code Material Notes
Black Mk1–Mk4 Not confirmed Leathercloth, black/grey carpet The default and most common interior. Pairs with all exterior colours. The Frogeye used an all-black interior across most of its production.
Red Mk2–Mk4 Not confirmed Leathercloth, black carpet Introduced with the Mk2. Paired with red exterior colours (Tartan Red, Flame Red).
Beige / Tan Mk3–Mk4 Not confirmed Leathercloth Available on some later Mk3 and Mk4 cars. Less common. Pairs with white or light-coloured exteriors.
ⓘ Frogeye note: The Mk1 Frogeye used a very simple interior — black leathercloth seats, basic black trim, no door cards (due to the absence of door handles), and a basic painted dash. Restoration to original spec is well-documented in club literature.
