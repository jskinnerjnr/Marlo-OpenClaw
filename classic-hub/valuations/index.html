Classic Car Price Guides

Condition-graded valuations and 15-year price trend charts for Triumph TR, Jaguar E-Type, Austin-Healey, MG and more. Updated annually. All figures in GBP — UK market.

Understanding Condition Grades
Concours
Restored to show standard or exceptional original condition. Correct in all details. Would win at national level.
Good Driver
Fully sorted and enjoyable. May have minor imperfections. Regularly used, well maintained. This is the sweet spot for most buyers.
Driver
Presentable and usable but with visible age. May need cosmetic attention. Mechanically sound.
Project
Requires restoration. May be running but not to a high standard. Priced for what it needs.

The Austin-Healey 3000 Mk3 BJ8 is the most desirable of the Big Healeys — wind-up windows, a more refined interior and the most powerful C-Series engine. Values have risen sharply since 2020. Matching-numbers cars with Heritage Certificates command the best money. Condition and provenance are everything.

What to look for when buying

  • Heritage Certificate — confirm matching numbers before purchase

The Triumph Spitfire is one of the most accessible classic sports cars on the market — huge club support, plentiful spares, and real usability. The 1500 is the most common and practical. Early Mk1 and Mk2 cars are rarer but more sought-after. Values rose sharply in 2021 and have settled at a healthier level than pre-COVID.

What to look for when buying
