Overview

The XK150 was the final development of Jaguar's post-war XK sports car line, introduced in 1957. It brought wider, more comfortable bodies, wind-up windows and significantly improved brakes — the XK150S was among the first production cars fitted with all-round disc brakes. The 3.8-litre engine option (from 1959) gave genuine 135mph capability. Replaced by the E-Type in 1961, the XK150 is now recognised as a fine grand tourer in its own right.

💡 S designation: XK150 S models (introduced 1958) have triple SU carburettors or straight-port cylinder heads and produce up to 250bhp. They command a significant premium over standard cars.
