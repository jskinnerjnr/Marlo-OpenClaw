Overview

The Jaguar Mk II is perhaps the most romantic saloon car ever made — a four-door car capable of 125mph (3.8L), with a beautiful interior of leather, walnut and Wilton carpet. It was the getaway car of choice for bank robbers and the favoured transport of the police chasing them. Today it is one of the most sought-after British classics, appreciated for its combination of beauty, refinement and performance.

💡 The 3.8 is king: The 3.8L Mk II with manual overdrive gearbox is the most desirable and valuable variant. Manual cars are significantly preferred over automatics. Wire wheels add value.

Opalescent metallic colours

Opalescent finishes were available at extra cost (typically ~£20–30 premium in period) and require specialist application for correct matching. The metallic particles in Jaguar opalescent paints produce a distinctive colour-shift depth effect not achievable with modern standard metallic paints.
