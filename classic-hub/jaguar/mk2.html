Overview

The Jaguar Mk II is perhaps the most romantic saloon car ever made — a four-door car capable of 125mph (3.8L), with a beautiful interior of leather, walnut and Wilton carpet. It was the getaway car of choice for bank robbers and the favoured transport of the police chasing them. Today it is one of the most sought-after British classics, appreciated for its combination of beauty, refinement and performance.

💡 The 3.8 is king: The 3.8L Mk II with manual overdrive gearbox is the most desirable and valuable variant. Manual cars are significantly preferred over automatics. Wire wheels add value.

Pairing guide
Old English White / Cream
→
White/cream suits any interior colour — maximum flexibility.
Opalescent Dark Blue / Indigo / Dark Blue
→
Blue body suits Biscuit for contrast or Blue for tone-on-tone. Both are correct.
Carmen Red / Imperial Maroon
→
Red or maroon body → Biscuit or Black interior. Red-on-red is non-standard.
Black
→
Black with red interior is the most dramatic combination; biscuit is more subtle.
Sherwood / Opalescent Dark Green
→
Green body with Biscuit is the quintessential Jaguar look; Red also works.
Opalescent Golden Sand / Bronze
→
Warm metallic bodies look best with Biscuit or Tan interior.

Colours shown are approximate digital representations. Always verify against a physical sample. Connolly hide is no longer produced — Bridge of Weir is the accepted modern equivalent for concours restoration.
