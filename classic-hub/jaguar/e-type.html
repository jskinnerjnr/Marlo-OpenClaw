Overview

Enzo Ferrari called it the most beautiful car in the world. The E-Type debuted at the 1961 Geneva Motor Show and caused a sensation — a 150mph grand tourer priced at £2,097. Over fourteen years and three series it evolved from the svelte Series 1 with its covered headlights and triple carburettors through the federalised Series 2 to the wide-tracked V12 Series 3. All remain among the most desirable classic cars in the world.

⚠️ Buyer's note: Early S1 cars (pre-1965) with flat floors, external bonnet latches and 3.8L engines command the highest premiums. Verify all numbers — Heritage Certificates from Jaguar Daimler Heritage Trust are strongly recommended.

Colour pairing guide
Old English White / Cream / Pearl Grey
→
White bodies suit any interior. Red/white is the most iconic E-Type combination.
Carmen Red / Regency Red / Signal Red
→
Red body → Biscuit or Black interior. Red-on-red is non-standard.
British Racing Green / Sherwood Green
→
BRG with Biscuit interior is the classic combination. Red interior on BRG is also correct.
Opalescent Dark Blue / Silver Blue
→
Blue body suits Biscuit or Blue leather. Blue-on-blue is rare and desirable.
Sable / Opalescent Bronze / Gunmetal
→
Dark body colours look best with Biscuit for contrast.
Azure Blue / Turquoise (S3)
→
S3 blues work well with Biscuit or the new Cinnamon option.

Colours shown are approximate digital representations of original factory specifications. Always verify against a physical sample. Original Connolly hide is no longer produced — Bridge of Weir hides are the closest modern equivalent.
