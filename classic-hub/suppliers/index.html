Which material for which car?

A quick reference guide to period-correct materials for classic British car interiors.

Material Period Correct for Notes
Connolly Hide 1920s–1980s Jaguar, Bentley, Rolls-Royce; many Triumph, Healey, MG Firm natural grain. Needs conditioning. Original supplier.
Ambla / Vynide Leathercloth 1950s–1970s MGB, Spitfire, Midget, Austin-Healey 3000, TR4/5/6 Vinyl-backed fabric. Period-correct for budget/standard trim levels.
P.V.C. Vinyl 1960s–1980s Later MGB, Midget, Spitfire rubber-bumper era Smoother, harder than Ambla. Common on later cars.
Mohair Soft Top 1960s–present MGB, TR6, Spitfire (later), all convertibles post-1965 German-woven mohair is the correct choice for post-1965 hoods.
Canvas Hoodcloth 1950s–1965 Austin-Healey 100/3000, early Sprite, TR2/3/4 Woven cotton canvas, not mohair. Important distinction.
Wool Loop Carpet 1950s–1970s Most British classics Correct for almost all period cars. Avoid cut-pile on older cars.
Hardura Rubber 1950s–early 1960s Austin-Healey 100, early MGA, TR2/TR3 Black rubber matting on floor — correct for early cars, not carpet.
Hessian Underlay All Under all carpets Traditional underlayer — gives correct feel.
