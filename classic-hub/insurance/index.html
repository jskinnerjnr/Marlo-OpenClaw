Classic Car Insurance

Specialist insurers who understand the value of your Triumph or Jaguar — and won't treat it like a Ford Fiesta when you need to claim.

Before you choose — what to check

  • Agreed value vs market value — always insist on agreed value. Market value policies let the insurer argue the figure down at claim time.
  • Salvage rights — make sure you retain the right to buy back a written-off car. Classic salvage has genuine value.
  • Laid-up cover — if you store for winter, a SORN/laid-up policy is much cheaper than full road cover.
  • Club membership discount — TR Register, JEC and JDC members get discounts with several of the insurers below. Join first, then get a quote.
  • Mileage limit — most classic policies have annual mileage limits (typically 3,000–7,500 miles). Declare your realistic usage honestly.
  • Modification disclosure — non-standard wheels, engine upgrades, roll bars — declare everything. Undisclosed mods void claims.
