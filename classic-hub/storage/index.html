Classic Car Storage Guide

From a dry lock-up to full concours facilities — what each tier costs, what it includes, and what to look for before you sign.

Storage Tiers
🔒
Level 1
Basic Indoor Storage
£30–80/month
A dry, lockable indoor space — the minimum for storing a classic car. Keeps off weather and (most) damp. Variable security standards.
✓ Dry indoor
✓ Lockable unit
Sometimes Alarmed
Unlikely CCTV
Sometimes 24hr access
✗ Climate control
✗ Monthly checks
✗ Battery maintenance
✗ Valet
Good for: Budget storage for a car that's running and regularly used. Not suitable for long-term or high-value cars.
Ask before signing:
  • Check for damp — view in winter or after rain
  • Ask about pest control
  • Confirm insurance covers off-road storage
🏢
Level 2
Managed Storage Facility
£80–180/month
Purpose-built storage with security and some facility management. The minimum recommended for unattended winter storage of a classic.
✓ Dry indoor
✓ Lockable unit
✓ Alarmed
✓ CCTV
Often 24hr access
✗ Climate control
Sometimes Monthly checks
Sometimes Battery maintenance
Sometimes Valet
Good for: Winter storage for most classics. Good balance of security and cost.
Ask before signing:
  • Check humidity levels — some 'secure' units are still damp
  • Confirm who has access and when
  • Read the contract for liability clauses
🌡️
Level 3
Premium Climate-Controlled Storage
£150–350/month
Temperature and humidity-controlled storage. Prevents the condensation cycles that cause rust and leather deterioration. Recommended for high-value or concours cars.
✓ Dry indoor
✓ Lockable unit
✓ Alarmed
✓ CCTV
✓ 24hr access
✓ Climate control
✓ Monthly checks
✓ Battery maintenance
Sometimes Valet
Good for: High-value classics, long-term storage (1+ years), concours-condition cars.
Ask before signing:
  • Ask about temperature and humidity targets — ideally 15–18°C / 50–55% RH
  • Check policy on visits and access
  • Confirm battery tender provision
🏆
Level 4
Concours / White-Glove Storage
£300–600+/month
Premium facilities offering museum-standard storage. Precision climate control, full vehicle preparation on collection, regular inspection reports and photography. For cars where cost is secondary to condition.
✓ Dry indoor
✓ Lockable unit
✓ Alarmed
✓ CCTV
✓ 24hr access
Precision Climate control
✓ + Report Monthly checks
✓ Battery maintenance
✓ Full Valet
Good for: Cars worth £50,000+, long-term storage, pre-sale preparation.
Ask before signing:
  • Prices can be negotiable on long-term contracts
  • Check insurance — some facilities have blanket cover included
  • Inspect the facility in person — presentation tells you a lot
Preparing Your Car for Storage

Before you hand over the keys

Change the oil before storage — used oil is acidic and will corrode engine internals over winter
Fill the fuel tank to prevent condensation — add a fuel stabiliser for storage over 3 months
Inflate tyres to the upper end of the recommended range — cars sitting deflated develop flat spots
Disconnect the battery or use a quality trickle charger (CTEK or similar) — modern chargers are fire-safe
Cover with a breathable cotton dust cover — never an airtight plastic cover, which traps moisture
Leave the windows slightly open (1cm) if the roof is sound — prevents musty interior
Place rodent deterrents (not poison) — mice are catastrophic for wiring looms and leather
Photograph and document condition before handing over to a storage facility
Check your insurance covers off-road storage — many standard policies exclude it
