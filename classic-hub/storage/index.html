Pre-storage preparation checklist

  • Fuel: Fill the tank and add fuel stabiliser (Sta-Bil or similar). A full tank prevents condensation and fuel degradation.
  • Oil change: Change the oil before storage — used oil contains acids that will corrode engine internals over winter.
  • Coolant: Check antifreeze strength is correct for the storage location's minimum temperature.
  • Battery: Disconnect or connect to a quality trickle charger (CTEK or similar). Never leave a classic battery to discharge fully.
  • Tyres: Inflate to 5–10 psi above normal to compensate for slow deflation. Use tyre savers or move the car monthly if possible.
  • Brakes: Leave the car in gear rather than applying the handbrake — brake discs and pads can seize if left applied for months.
  • Wash and wax: Clean thoroughly before storage — dirt and bird lime are corrosive over time. Apply a quality wax.
  • Moisture control: Use a breathable car cover (never plastic). Place moisture absorbers (DampRid/Damp Trap) inside.
  • Rodent protection: Block exhaust and air intake with steel wool or foam. Mice love classic car interiors.
  • Insurance: Check your policy covers storage — or switch to a cheaper SORN/laid-up policy for the winter months.
  • Documentation: Leave a note with the car including your contact details and any known quirks for facility staff.
