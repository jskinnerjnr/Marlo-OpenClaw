Materials & Colours

Your colour and material choices are the starting point for a beautiful interior.

We break our materials into Primary and Secondary. Primary materials — leather and vinyl — determine the dominant colour of your interior. Secondary materials — carpets, moquette, piping — complement your primary choice. Our range is the most comprehensive in the industry.

Primary Material

Vinyl & Leathercloth

Vinyl is the factory-correct leathercloth used on seats, door cards, dashboards and hood interiors across the majority of classic British sports cars. A high-quality PVC-coated fabric that replicates the look of leather — hardwearing, easy to clean and available in a comprehensive range of period colours.

Leathercloth is a softer, more supple alternative to standard vinyl — with a slightly different surface texture that more closely mimics the feel of leather. Used in specific applications and models where a finer finish is required. Available in a broad but more selective colour range.

Carpet

Wool carpet is the premium choice — the factory-correct material for the majority of classic British sports cars. Tightly woven from natural wool fibre, it is hard-wearing, sound-deadening and gives the interior that distinctive period smell and feel that synthetic alternatives cannot match.

Secondary Material

Hardura & Armacord

Hardura is the durable rubber-backed floor covering used in footwells, transmission tunnels and other high-wear areas of the classic car interior. Its firm, textured surface withstands decades of use and is the factory-correct specification for a wide range of British classics.

Armacord is the characteristic ribbed flexible material used in hoods and tonneau covers on many classic British sports cars — factory-correct for MGA, Triumph TR and Austin Healey. Its distinctive ribbed texture is immediately recognisable and available in all original factory colours.

Secondary Material

Binding & Piping

Binding is the woven tape used to finish carpet edges in the correct period style — covering raw edges cleanly and neatly. Applied by machine in a matching or complementary colour, it is a key detail in any concours-quality carpet kit and distinguishes a professional finish from an amateur one.

Piping is the fine welt sewn into seat seams and carpet edges — the defining finishing detail of a classic British interior. Choose a matching colour for a clean, unified look, or select a contrasting colour for a period two-tone effect.

Secondary Material

Moquette & Furflex

Moquette is the distinctive looped or cut-pile fabric used on seat facings and door panels in specific classic British car models. Factory-spec in the correct pile height and weave, it provides a unique tactile quality that no substitute material can replicate.

Furflex is the channel-based draught-excluding material used on door frames, hood frames and apertures throughout the classic car interior. A critical detail in any concours or road-going restoration — missing or incorrect furflex is immediately obvious and affects both appearance and comfort.

Colour Matching

Can't find your exact colour?

Our colour matching service allows us to supply materials in colours not part of our standard range. We can work from original trim samples, paint references, or photographs to match colours as closely as possible.

Particularly useful for two-tone interiors, non-standard factory options, and restorations where the car's history demands a specific colour combination. Contact us to discuss your colour matching requirement.
