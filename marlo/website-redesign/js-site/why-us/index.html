Our Advantages

Why the world's best restorations choose John Skinner

🪡

Original Specification Patterns

Every pattern reverse-engineered from genuine, original factory trim — not after-market replica. Four decades of pattern archive, refined by customer feedback and professional trimmer input. When concours judges scrutinise your interior, these details are what matter.

🏭

Made in Cheltenham

Cut, sewn and trimmed in our own workshop by skilled craftspeople. We don't outsource a single piece. Every kit that leaves our doors was made by our team, on our machines, in our factory. British manufacturing. Not a badge we wear — a commitment we keep.

🎯

Deep Marque Expertise

We make trim for Jaguar, Triumph TR, Austin Healey and MG — and nothing else. That deliberate focus means our knowledge of these specific cars is unmatched. We know which direction the ribs run in an Austin Healey. We know the exact weld pattern on a TR6 seat. That's the difference.

🎨

Widest Colour & Material Range

The most comprehensive library of trim materials and colours available in the industry — all in-house. Leather in three finishes, Connolly Vaumol™, wool carpet in 20+ colours, Karvel, Hardura, Moquette. If it originally went in these cars, we have it.

🔬

Precision CNC Cutting

Modern technology serving traditional craft. Our digital CNC machine cuts every piece with millimetre accuracy — notches, drill holes, shaped curves. The kind of consistency that handcutting simply cannot achieve. Better fit, every time.

🤝

Personal Expert Guidance

Every enquiry handled by someone who genuinely knows these cars. We'll guide your colour choices, advise on originality, and be on hand throughout the process — from first call to fitting questions. You connect with us directly. Always.

🌍

Ships Worldwide

Our kits travel from Cheltenham to customers across the UK, Europe, North America, Australia and beyond. Classic car restoration is a global pursuit — and we've been supplying it globally for decades.

🏆

Award-Winning Results

Our trim has featured in Concours d'Elegance winners, perfect-score judging results, F1 Grand Prix parade cars, and royal commissions. When the stakes are highest, restorers choose John Skinner.
