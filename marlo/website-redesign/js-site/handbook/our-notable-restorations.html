Notable Restorations

Our Notable Restorations

From the Jaguar E-Type Cut 7 to the first production Triumph TR TS01, we have been the choice for many of the most historic and high-profile restorations in the classic car world.

← Back to Handbook

Our interiors have been featured in 'perfect score' Concours d'Elegance award winners and appeared throughout F1 Grand Prix Drivers' Parades. With our worldwide reputation, we are known throughout the industry to provide the expertise, in-depth knowledge and attention to detail that these historic and high-profile restorations demand.

King Carl XVI Gustaf's Daimler Princess

In 2018, we were extremely privileged to be contacted to contribute Grey Wool Carpet material towards the restoration of the Daimler Princess belonging to the King of Sweden, Carl XVI Gustaf. Our wool carpet is, quite literally, fit for a king.

Richard Hammond's Jaguar XK150 FHC

In the summer of 2020, we were approached by Neil & Anthony Greenhouse of Tram Inn Garage in Herefordshire who were undertaking the extensive restoration of Richard Hammond's Jaguar XK150 FHC. We manufactured a complete interior trim kit in Smooth Cherry Red Leather, which included a bespoke fire extinguisher cover. An exciting project for everyone at John Skinner — especially when we saw Jonathon and our factory featured on DriveTribe. Read the full story →

Jaguar E-Type Cut 7

In 2016, we supplied a full trim kit for the restoration of the second Jaguar E-Type FHC Cut 7 — the car famously driven, and crashed, by Dick Protheroe. The full trim kit consisted of Vinyl Panels and LeatherFaced Seats in Midnight Blue with Dark Blue Wool Carpet and Hardura.

Triumph TR2 'Jabbeke'

In 2016, we worked in collaboration with Protek Engineering in their ground-up restoration of the Triumph TR2 'Jabbeke' — renowned as the initial Triumph TR prototype that held the world land speed record. We supplied Trim Panels in Midnight Blue Vinyl alongside LeatherFaced Seats and a Grey Wool Carpet. The car was exhibited at the Royal Automobile Club, Pall Mall, London in March 2017.

Triumph TR2 TS01

In 2013, we supplied interior trim items to Mark Macy of Macy's Garage, Ohio, USA, for his restoration of the Triumph TR2 TS1 — the first left hand drive Triumph TR to leave the production line in 1953. We manufactured the Carpet Kit and Boot Trunk Carpet in Bright Red Wool for this prominent restoration.

TR4 Perfect 100 Point Score

John Skinner's proudly supplied Mark Macy with interior trim parts for his 1961 Triumph TR4 Surrey Top restoration, which went on to scoop Best of Show at the national meet of the Triumph Register of America (TRA) in 2018, achieving the first ever 100 Point Score awarded by the TRA.
