The Project

When Paul Kilvington — the man who defined the Range Rover's positioning for its North American launch — decided to create an ultra-limited restomod edition, he came to John Skinner for the interior. That's not a coincidence. It's a recognition that if you're going to reimagine one of the most iconic 4x4s ever built, only one trimshop in Britain has the knowledge, patterns and craft to do the interior justice.

The Kilvington Classic takes a Series 1 Classic Range Rover and subjects it to a complete, ground-up restoration and reengineering. Every panel, every seam, every detail is reviewed. The interior is no different.

"If you're building something at this level, the interior has to be as considered as the bodywork. There's nowhere to hide in a Range Rover cabin."

Our Contribution

John Skinner (Manufacturing) Ltd supplied the complete interior trim package for the Kilvington Classic programme. Working closely with the Kilvington team, we developed the seat covers, door cards, carpet and headlining to a specification that honours the original's character while meeting the expectations of a £189,000-plus commission.

Materials were selected for their authenticity to the period as well as their longevity — Connolly-style leather, period-correct wool carpet, and handmade detail at every junction. Every kit was cut and sewn in our Cheltenham workshop, as it always has been.

About Kilvington Classic

The Kilvington Classic programme was conceived by Paul Kilvington, whose career began with a commission from Land Rover itself — to define the brand positioning and design the launch strategy for the Range Rover's entry into North America. That work ignited a lifelong obsession with what makes the Classic Range Rover truly special.

Limited to just 200 examples worldwide, each Kilvington Classic is an individually commissioned project. No two are identical. The base vehicle is a genuine Series 1 Classic Range Rover, fully stripped and rebuilt to a specification that surpasses the original in every measurable way — while preserving the soul that made it iconic in the first place.

For more information about the Kilvington Classic programme, visit kilvingtonclassic.com.
