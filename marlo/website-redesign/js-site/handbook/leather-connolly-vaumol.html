Leather: Connolly Vaumol™

Connolly is a recognised and admired name amongst classic car restorers. With a steeped history in automotive upholstery, here we take a closer look at the leather behind the brand.

← Back to Handbook

Established in 1878 by the Connolly brothers — John Joseph and Samuel Frederick — Connolly Leather was firmly rooted in the supply of leather to the horse-centred transport industry of the time: saddlery, hooding and coach building. So when horse-drawn coaches evolved into the first motor cars, the Connolly Brothers quickly found themselves at the very centre of the golden age of motoring.

Connolly leather graced the interiors of the first Rolls Royce, Bentley, Aston Martin and Jaguar amongst many others, and continued to supply the leather to these cars up until the 1990s.

From the seats in the House of Lords and House of Commons, to Concorde, the QE2 and the Ritz hotels, Connolly leather was chosen to bedeck the interiors of some of the most prestigious and esteemed settings in British history.

It's this heritage that makes Connolly a recognised and admired name amongst today's classic car restorers. More than just leather, the Connolly brand recalls and captures a moment in a by-gone era that restorers are increasingly keen to revisit and relive.

Modern-Day Leather Manufacturing

Over the years, leather manufacturing has evolved to meet the demands of the mass market by increasing its usability and prolonging its lifespan. Through mechanical graining processes and dye treatments, leather hides can be made more consistent in their look and feel, thereby increasing their suitability for mass production.

Connolly Vaumol™ takes a different approach — celebrating the unique natural qualities of each hide through graining, patina, aroma and handfeel. The result is a leather that feels authentic, ages beautifully, and connects the restorer to the original character of these cars.

Why We Offer Connolly Vaumol™

For those who want the most authentic possible interior restoration, Connolly Vaumol™ is the natural choice. We offer it across our full range of trim kits as an upgrade option — bringing the original leather supplier back to the cars it was made for.
