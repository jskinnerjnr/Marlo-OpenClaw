The TR6 front seat cover kit is one of our most popular products — and for good reason. The TR6 is a beautiful car, and a well-trimmed interior is the difference between a car that looks finished and one that looks restored.

Every kit is cut from the original factory patterns that we've held and refined since we first made TR6 trim in 1980. The fit is exact. We know where the seams go, we know the correct piping profile, and we know the small details that make the difference between a trim job that looks right and one that doesn't.

The kit covers both front seats — driver and passenger. Squab covers, backrest covers, and all associated finishing panels are included. Available in vinyl, leather-faced (a combination of leather on the seat faces with vinyl on the back and sides) or full leather throughout.

Our colour range is the most comprehensive in the industry. If you want to match the original factory colour exactly, we can do that. If you want to do something slightly different — contrasting piping, a custom colour combination — we can discuss that too. The starting point is always a conversation.

We strongly recommend ordering physical samples before confirming your colour choice. What looks one way on a screen is often quite different in person, and in natural light. Samples are free.

Note on seat foams: Seat foam and wadding are not included in this kit — they are available separately. If your original foams are in good condition, these covers will fit over them. If you need replacement foams, add them to your quote and we'll include them.

These kits are designed for the capable enthusiast — you don't need to be a professional trimmer to fit them, but you do need patience, the right tools, and a willingness to take your time. If you've never trimmed before, read through our Handbook fitting guides first.

1

Remove the seats from the car

Unbolt the seats and remove completely. It's much easier to trim seats on a workbench than in the car.

2

Strip the old covers

Remove existing covers carefully. Note how they're attached — hog rings, clips, or both. This helps when fitting the new covers.

3

Check the foam condition

Inspect the seat foams while the covers are off. If they've collapsed or broken down, now is the time to replace them.

4

Fit the new covers

Work from the centre outward. Don't rush — pull the cover evenly to avoid creasing. Use the hog ring pliers to secure where indicated.

5

Refit to car

Once happy with the fit, refit the seats to the car and check the finished result in situ.
