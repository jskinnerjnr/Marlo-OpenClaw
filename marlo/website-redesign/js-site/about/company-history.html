About / Company History

Est. 1980

It all started with a TR6.

John Skinner didn't set out to build a business. He set out to fix his car. Forty-five years later, that restless pursuit of knowledge has become one of the world's most respected interior trim manufacturers.

Early 1970s

A TR6 and a problem

John Skinner bought a Triumph TR6 — and quickly discovered the cost of keeping one on the road. Rather than pay for repairs he didn't understand, John set about learning. Through buying, selling and dismantling many Triumphs, he accumulated parts, contacts, and an increasingly encyclopaedic knowledge of how these cars were made.

1980

A hobby becomes a business

Spotting an opportunity for TR4/TR6 trim re-manufacture, John invested in tooling for trim panel production. His products were well received by UK-based parts suppliers. By 1980, the side project had grown enough to justify leaving his full-time job. John Skinner (Manufacturing) Ltd was born — in a garage.

Mid 1980s

Moving to Cirencester

When the garage became too small, John relocated to a factory unit in Cirencester. The product range expanded — Jaguar XK, E-Type, MK2 through XJ-S Saloon trim was added, followed by Austin Healey. Each new marque brought new patterns, new knowledge, new customers from further afield.

1990s–2000s

An international reputation, quietly built

The business grew steadily on word of mouth and an uncompromising commitment to quality. Customers from Australia, the USA, Germany and beyond began placing orders. The workshop relocated to Cheltenham, and the team grew around skilled craftspeople who treated every kit as their own.

2010s

The second generation

John's son Jonathon joined the business, bringing new energy. CNC cutting technology was introduced, digital patterns refined, and the company's online presence built out. Crucially, none of it came at the cost of the values John had established: accuracy, quality, and genuine expertise.

2020

Richard Hammond's XK150

During lockdown, Richard Hammond called. His XK150 FHC restoration — undertaken by Tram Inn Garage in Herefordshire — needed an interior. He chose John Skinner. The Cherry Red interior became one of the most celebrated recent restorations in the classic car world, featured on DriveTribe.

Today

World-class, still family-run

Jonathon Skinner and his wife Lucy manage the business from Cheltenham. John himself remains on-hand for technical advice. The team is small, skilled, and deeply invested in every kit that leaves the building. Some staff members have been here for close to a decade. The standard has never dropped.

"Two generations of passion, skill and hard work have brought us to where we are today — a world leader in quality interior trim and upholstery manufacture."

Jonathon Skinner, Managing Director
