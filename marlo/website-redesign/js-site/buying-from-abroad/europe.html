Continental Europe · Post-Brexit Guide

🇪🇺 Buying from the EU

Brexit changed the paperwork — not the quality, not the price. Our products are 100% UK-made and attract zero duties to the EU. Here's exactly how it works.

The Bottom Line

Don't Let Brexit Put You Off

We continue to supply restoration kits to customers across Germany, France, the Netherlands, Belgium, Spain, Italy, Scandinavia and beyond. Brexit has not changed what you pay in total — it has only changed the process slightly.

Understanding the Tax

Where the Tax Goes — and Why It Moves

In the UK, every purchase includes 20% VAT built into the price. When you buy from us as a UK customer, you're paying that 20% to us, and we pass it to HMRC.

Because you're outside the UK VAT area, we do not charge you UK VAT. But that doesn't mean the tax disappears — it simply moves. Instead of paying tax to us at the point of sale, you pay the equivalent tax to your own government when the goods arrive at your border. The tax is owed; it just goes to your country, not ours.

What you pay on arrival is your country's local import VAT rate — the same rate you'd pay on any domestic purchase. In Germany that's 19%, France 20%, the Netherlands 21%. For most customers, the total cost of the order is virtually identical to what it would have been before Brexit.

Key fact: UK-manufactured goods exported to the EU attract 0% import duty under the UK–EU Trade and Cooperation Agreement (TCA). You pay your local VAT only — no additional tariffs or duties on top. We supply full UK origin documentation with every shipment to confirm this at customs.

DDP Shipping

We Handle the Complexity

For most EU countries, we offer DDP (Delivered Duty Paid) shipping. This means all import charges are settled at the point of your order — you pay us once, and your kit arrives at your door without any additional payment required at the border.

Where DDP is not available for your specific country, local customs will contact you on arrival to arrange your local VAT payment directly with them. This is a simple, standard process — our documentation ensures there are no delays and no unexpected charges.

We email you a copy of your shipping invoice and all customs documentation the moment your kit leaves our factory. You have full visibility of everything reviewed at customs — no surprises.

Common Questions

FAQ — EU Customers

Will I have to pay import duties on my kit?
No. Our products are manufactured in the United Kingdom. Under the UK–EU Trade and Cooperation Agreement, UK-origin goods attract 0% tariff when imported into EU member states. We supply full UK origin documentation and commercial invoices with every shipment specifically to confirm this.
Why is my order cheaper than before Brexit?
Because we no longer charge you UK VAT (20%) at the point of sale — you are outside the UK VAT area, so we are not permitted to collect it on the UK government's behalf. That doesn't mean the tax is waived. It means your own country's government collects their equivalent tax when the goods arrive at your border. If your local rate is lower than 20% (Germany is 19%), your total cost is slightly less. If it's the same (France is 20%), it's virtually identical. Either way, you're not being taxed twice — the tax simply moves.
What is DDP shipping and do you offer it?
DDP (Delivered Duty Paid) means all import charges are settled upfront at the time of your order — you pay once and your kit arrives without any further payment required at the border. We offer DDP to most EU countries. Contact us when requesting a quote and we will confirm availability for your country.
What happens at customs if DDP isn't available for my country?
Your local customs authority will contact you to arrange payment of your country's import VAT. This is a straightforward, standard process. Because our documentation confirms 0% duty, the only charge is your local VAT. The small admin fee (typically €10–€15) is charged by the customs authority or courier. We provide everything your customs office needs to process the shipment quickly.
How long will delivery take?
Most full interior kits take 8–12 weeks in production. Once dispatched, European delivery is typically 2–5 working days via FedEx, DHL, UPS or DPD. We can arrange part-shipments on larger orders so your restoration can begin before the full kit is complete.
What currency can I pay in?
We accept payment by credit or debit card, UK bank transfer, or Wise (formerly TransferWise). Wise allows you to pay in your local currency with transparent exchange rates — it is often the most cost-effective option for our European customers and is our preferred method for international transfers.
