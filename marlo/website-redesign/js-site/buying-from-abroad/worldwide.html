Where We Ship

We've Shipped Everywhere a Classic Has Been Driven

Classic British cars were exported in enormous numbers throughout the 1950s, 60s and 70s. Jaguar E-Types, Triumph TRs, Austin Healeys and MGBs went to Australia, New Zealand, South Africa, Japan, South America and the Middle East. Those cars are still there — still being restored — and we are still the right people to trim them.

In recent years we have shipped to Australia, New Zealand, South Africa, Japan, Vietnam, Brazil, Israel, Lebanon, Jordan and many more. Every country has its own import rules and duties. We will always be straightforward with you about what applies to your order.

🇦🇺

Australia

One of our most active markets outside of North America and Europe. Australian restorers have exceptional access to surviving British cars. GST applies on import. We are experienced at shipping to all Australian states.

What to Expect

How International Shipping Works

The process is the same regardless of where you are in the world. We handle everything up to the point your shipment crosses your country's border — from there, local import rules apply.

📋

Full Documentation

Every international shipment leaves with a commercial invoice, packing list, and UK origin certificate. This is everything your customs authority needs. We email copies to you before dispatch.

📦

Couriers We Use

FedEx, DHL, UPS and DPD — all with full tracking and insurance to replacement value. We select the most reliable option for your destination based on our experience.

🏭

Production Lead Time

Most full interior kits take 8–12 weeks in production. We update you throughout. Part-shipments are available for larger orders so your restoration can begin before everything is complete.

💳

Payment

Card, UK bank transfer, or Wise. Wise is our preferred method for international customers — pay in your local currency at the real exchange rate with no hidden fees.

🛃

Duties & Taxes — How It Works

We do not charge UK VAT on international orders — you are outside the UK VAT area. That doesn't mean the tax disappears. Your own country collects its equivalent tax (GST, import VAT, customs duty) when your shipment arrives at the border. What you owe depends on your country's rates and the declared value of the goods. We will always tell you what to expect at the time of quoting, and provide full documentation to ensure fast clearance.

Not sure what applies to your country? Contact us with your location when requesting a quote and we'll give you a clear picture of what to expect on duties, taxes and delivery timeline before you commit to anything.
