USA & North America · Buying Guide

🇺🇸 Buying from the USA & Canada

North America is our largest export market. We've been shipping to customers across the USA and Canada for decades. Here's everything you need to know — including our honest take on tariffs.

The Bottom Line

We've Been Doing This for Decades

The United States and Canada represent our single largest export market. We have been shipping interior trim kits to American and Canadian restorers since the 1980s — Jaguar E-Types in California, Triumph TRs in Texas, Austin Healeys in New England. The classic car community in North America is passionate, knowledgeable and exacting — and they keep coming back to us because the quality of British-made trim simply isn't available at home.

Shipping internationally does involve some paperwork and occasional duties, but it is a routine process. Our experienced team handles international shipments every week. Here is exactly what to expect.

Understanding the Tax

No UK VAT — But Tax Is Still Owed

In the UK, 20% VAT is included in the price of most goods. When you buy from us as a US or Canadian customer, we do not charge you UK VAT — you are outside the UK VAT area and we are not permitted to collect it on the UK government's behalf.

But that doesn't mean the tax disappears. It moves. Instead of paying tax to us at point of sale, any applicable sales tax or import tax is assessed by US Customs and Border Protection (or the Canada Border Services Agency) when your shipment enters the country. What you owe depends on the declared value of the goods and the applicable duty and tax rates for your shipment category.

For low-value shipments entering the USA, the de minimis threshold (currently $800) means no duty or tax is assessed at all. For larger orders, import duty and any applicable state-level use tax may apply. We will always give you a clear picture of what to expect when we quote.

Tariffs & Duties

An Honest Word on Tariffs

We won't pretend tariffs don't exist — you deserve a straight answer. Our products are classified under HS code 8708.99 (other parts and accessories for motor vehicles). Historically this category has attracted a low or zero duty rate into the USA.

The current tariff landscape is evolving. We are monitoring the situation closely and we are committed to being transparent with every customer about what applies to their specific order at the time of quoting. We will never hide a cost from you.

✓ What Works in Your Favour

Classic car interior trim components are specialist, low-volume goods. There is no American equivalent — these kits are made in Britain and only available from specialists like us. The cost of the kit and the quality of the restoration are what matter most to serious collectors. A modest duty does not change that calculation.

How We Can Help

For larger orders, we can discuss splitting your order across multiple shipments over time — a legitimate approach that some customers use to spread costs and manage cash flow. We will always provide accurate, honest documentation and will never misrepresent the nature of a shipment. For complex situations we recommend consulting a licensed customs broker.

Our position: We will absorb cost wherever we reasonably can. We will be transparent about what applies at the time of your order. We have never lost a long-standing American customer over a duty charge, and we don't intend to start. The mainstream coverage of tariffs is often more alarming than the reality for specialist goods like ours.

Common Questions

FAQ — USA & Canada

Will I have to pay import duty on my kit?
Possibly, depending on the current tariff schedule and the value of your order. Our products are classified under HS 8708.99 (motor vehicle parts). Historically this category has attracted a low or zero duty rate into the USA, but the tariff landscape is currently evolving. We will give you an accurate picture of what applies at the time of quoting.
How do Trump's tariffs affect my order?
We understand this is a concern for many of our American customers. The situation is being closely monitored and we are transparent with every customer about current rates. In practice, for specialist classic car trim components — which cannot be sourced domestically — a duty charge has not deterred serious collectors from completing their restorations. We will always tell you exactly what to expect before you commit to an order.
Can I split my order across multiple shipments?
Yes, this is something we offer and some customers find it useful both for managing duty costs and for starting their restoration before the full kit is complete. We can ship seat covers first, for example, while carpet and headlining are still in production. Any splitting of orders is done with full, accurate documentation — we do not misrepresent shipments.
How long will my order take?
Most full interior kits take 8–12 weeks in production. Once dispatched, delivery to the continental USA is typically 3–5 working days via FedEx, DHL or UPS. We can often turn around smaller or partial orders more quickly.
Is my shipment insured?
Yes, every international shipment is insured to full replacement value. In the unlikely event of loss or damage in transit, you are fully covered. We have shipped thousands of kits internationally and issues in transit are extremely rare.
Can I pay in US dollars?
Via Wise, yes — you can pay in USD and the conversion is handled transparently at the real exchange rate. Card payments are processed in GBP but your card issuer will handle the conversion. Bank transfers are accepted in GBP or USD.
Do you have American customers who can vouch for the process?
We have hundreds of American and Canadian customers. We'd encourage you to read our testimonials — many are from North American restorers who have been through the full process from quote to installation.
