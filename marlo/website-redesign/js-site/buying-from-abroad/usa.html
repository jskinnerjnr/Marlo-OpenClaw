USA & North America · Buying Guide

🇺🇸 Buying from the USA & Canada

North America is our single largest export market. Thousands of American and Canadian restorers have bought from us — and they keep coming back. Here is how the process works, clearly explained.

Why North America Buys British

There Is No American Equivalent

We have been shipping interior trim kits to American and Canadian restorers since the 1980s — Jaguar E-Types in California, Triumph TRs in Texas, Austin Healeys in New England. The classic car community in North America is serious, knowledgeable and demanding. They buy from us because there is simply nothing available domestically that comes close.

Even accounting for shipping costs and any applicable import tax, a John Skinner kit is frequently competitive with — or cheaper than — what you'd pay a local trimmer to attempt the same work. And the end result isn't comparable. This is the original specification, made by the people who have been making it since before most of your local competition existed.

Understanding the Tax

No UK VAT — Here's What That Means

In the UK, every purchase includes 20% VAT in the price. Because you're outside the UK VAT area, we do not charge you that tax. Your invoice is lower as a result — the UK government's share is simply not collected.

Instead, US Customs and Border Protection (or Canada Border Services Agency) may assess import duty and/or tax when your shipment enters the country. The rate depends on the declared value and the applicable tariff category for your goods. For many orders, particularly lower-value shipments, the assessment is minimal or zero. We will always tell you clearly what to expect when we quote.

Tariffs & Duties

A Clear-Eyed View on Tariffs

Our products are classified under HS code 8708.99 (parts and accessories for motor vehicles). We will tell you clearly at the time of quoting what duty rate applies to your order — no hidden costs, no surprises.

What we can say with confidence: the people who buy from us are not buying on price alone. They are buying the right kit — the one made to the original specification, in the right materials, by the right people. A modest duty charge has never stopped a serious restorer from getting what they need. It simply gets factored in alongside everything else.

Still the Best Value Overall

Even with shipping and any applicable duties, a John Skinner kit is frequently competitive with what a domestic trimmer would charge for inferior work. You are comparing a purpose-made, factory-quality interior kit with a hand-trimmer's best attempt. They are not the same thing — and most restorers know it.

Part-Shipments for Larger Orders

For larger kits, we can ship completed items as they're finished — seats first, then carpet, then headlining. This spreads the cost across multiple shipments over time, helps you manage cash flow, and gets your restoration moving sooner.

Common Questions

FAQ — USA & Canada

Will I have to pay import duty on my kit?
Possibly — it depends on the value of your order and the current tariff schedule for HS 8708.99 (motor vehicle parts). We will give you a clear, accurate figure when we quote. No surprises, no hidden costs.
How do Trump's tariffs affect my order?
We are transparent with every customer about what applies to their order. In practice, for specialist trim components that cannot be sourced anywhere else, a duty charge simply gets factored in alongside everything else — the same way any serious restorer factors in the cost of the right parts. We will tell you exactly what to expect before you commit to anything.
Can I split my order across multiple shipments?
Yes, this is something we offer and some customers find it useful both for managing duty costs and for starting their restoration before the full kit is complete. We can ship seat covers first, for example, while carpet and headlining are still in production. Any splitting of orders is done with full, accurate documentation — we do not misrepresent shipments.
How long will my order take?
Most full interior kits take 8–12 weeks in production. Once dispatched, delivery to the continental USA is typically 3–5 working days via FedEx, DHL or UPS. We can often turn around smaller or partial orders more quickly.
Is my shipment insured?
Yes, every international shipment is insured to full replacement value. In the unlikely event of loss or damage in transit, you are fully covered. We have shipped thousands of kits internationally and issues in transit are extremely rare.
Can I pay in US dollars?
Via Wise, yes — you can pay in USD and the conversion is handled transparently at the real exchange rate. Card payments are processed in GBP but your card issuer will handle the conversion. Bank transfers are accepted in GBP or USD.
Do you have American customers who can vouch for the process?
We have hundreds of American and Canadian customers. We'd encourage you to read our testimonials — many are from North American restorers who have been through the full process from quote to installation.
